# **Strategic Framework**

The AI Strategy Programme is structured around five strategic pillars, underpinned by four cross-cutting themes and enabled by six core AI capabilities. This framework ensures coherent investment prioritisation, capability development, and value realisation across the portfolio.

## **Strategic Pillars**

Five pillars define the primary value streams and strategic focus areas for AI investment:

| CLIENT EXCELLENCE | SALES & GROWTH | OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY | KNOWLEDGE & ADVISORY | DATA FOUNDATION |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |

| Pillar | Strategic Intent | Capability Domains |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Client Excellence | Deliver superior client understanding, engagement, and retention through AI-powered intelligence and proactive service delivery | Client Intelligence & Analytics, Renewals & Client Retention |
| 2\. Sales & Growth | Accelerate revenue growth through intelligent prospecting, lead optimisation, and AI-enhanced sales enablement | Prospecting & Sales Development, Campaigns |
| 3\. Operational Efficiency | Automate high-volume, repetitive processes to reduce costs, improve accuracy, and free capacity for value-adding activities | Quotes & Underwriting, Claims Processing, Reporting & Administration, Policy & Compliance |
| 4\. Knowledge & Advisory | Augment workforce expertise with AI-powered knowledge assistants, training tools, and decision support systems | Virtual AI Agents, AI Tools & Enablers, Risk Analysis & Due Diligence |
| 5\. Data Foundation | Establish robust data infrastructure, governance, and integration capabilities to enable all AI initiatives | Infrastructure & Data Platforms, Communications & Productivity |

## **Cross-Cutting Themes**

Four themes span all pillars and inform capability development, ensuring coherent architecture and maximised reuse:

| Data-Driven Decisions | Intelligent Automation | Workforce Augmentation | Compliance by Design |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Embed analytics and AI-generated insights into decision points across all business processes | Apply AI to automate routine tasks, reduce manual effort, and improve process consistency | Enhance human capabilities with AI assistants, tools, and knowledge systems | Build governance, audit, and regulatory compliance into AI solutions from inception |

## **RCSG Governance Layer**

Risk, Compliance, Security & Governance (RCSG) operates as a cross-cutting governance function spanning all five strategic pillars. Rather than a standalone investment pillar, RCSG ensures compliance-by-design, AI governance, and continuous assurance across the programme.

| RCSG Domain | Scope | Primary BSC Impact |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Enterprise Risk | Unified risk register aggregating findings from ALZ, O365, PP, TP audit workstreams | P3: Compliance, F4: Cost-to-Serve |
| Regulatory Compliance | Automated mapping across MCSB, NIST, ISO, FCA SYSC 13.9, PRA SS1/21, DORA, Lloyd's MS13 | P3: Compliance |
| Threat Modelling | STRIDE/DREAD methodology with MITRE ATT&CK/D3FEND alignment | P3: Compliance |
| AI Governance | UK AI Regulation, EU AI Act risk classification, OWASP LLM Top 10 for virtual agents | P3: Compliance, C2: Client Experience |
| Continuous Assurance | Drift detection, remediation tracking, trend analysis dashboards | P3: Compliance, F4: Cost-to-Serve |
| Governance Operating Model | RACI, policy lifecycle, exception management, regulatory change impact | All perspectives |
